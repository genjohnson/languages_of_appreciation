The 5 Languages of Appreciation

  • Words of affirmation
  • Quality time
  • Acts of service
  • Tangible gifts
  • Physical touch
What makes one person feel appreciated does not necessarily make another person feel appreciated.
We typically find it easiest to speak our own primary language, which may not resonate with others.

Words of Affirmation

Dialects

  • Praise for accomplishments
  • Affirmation for character
  • Praise for personality

Accomplishments: most common dialect in the workplace; effective verbal praise is specific (job well done)

Character: looks beyond performance and focuses on the inner nature of a person (trustworthy, acts with integrity)

Personality: words that focus on positive personality traits (orderly, optimistic)

Where & How

Personal vs. In Front of Others

Written vs. Verbal

Examples:
  • Verbal: In personal conversation vs. team meeting
  • Written: direct email vs. OneIT Thank You

Considerations

  • Preferences of the recipient
  • Be sincere & be specific

Quality Time

By "Quality time", we mean giving someone your focused attention

Dialects

  • Quality conversation
  • Shared experiences
  • Small group conversation
  • Close physical proximity
    to coworkers on a project
Quality conversation: most common dialect; sharing thoughts, feelings, desires in a friendly, uninterrupted context
  • Maintain eye contact
  • Don't do other things
  • Listen for feelings as well as thoughts
  • Affirm their feelings (even if you disagree with their conclusions)
  • Observe body language
  • Resist the urge to interrupt
Shared experiences: Often not 1-1; e.g., attend a conferences, out to eat, other shared interests
Small groups: Not everyone is comfortable with 1-1's, but appreciates attentive listening in a group setting
Pysical proximity: engage in conversation + accomplishing something worthwhile = feeling valued

Considerations

  • Different relationships
    with supervisor vs. coworkers
  • Be creative about finding time
  • Don't just go through the motions
Relationships: Some people may be comfortable spending quality time with coworkers but not supervisors
Finding time: We all have commitments outside of work. Maybe meet briefly before or after work, or over lunch.
Be sincere: Be present emotionally as well as physically (be on time, don't complain, have positive attitute)

  • Ask before you help
